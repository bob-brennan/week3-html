  • All sloths have three toes (digits) on each rear limb
  • They also have poor hearing
  • The two versus three toed name indicates the number of digits on each forelimb
  • Sloths have colour vision, but have poor visual acuity
  • Sloths are tropical mammals that live in Central and South America
  • They rely on their sense of smell and touch to find food
  • Two-toed sloths are omnivorous, meaning they can consume plants and animals
  • Three-toed sloths, on the other hand, are almost entirely herbivorous
  • The outer hairs of sloth fur grow away from their extremities
  • Green algae, known as Trichophilus grows on their fur. It becomes more pronounced during the rainy season
  • A sloth has less muscle per pound that any other mammal
  • It can tale up to 30 days to digest a single meal
  • Sloths move slowly to conserve energy
  • Sloths have special bone structure used to grip, hold and hang
  • Sloths are excellent swimmers
  • Sloths sleep about 15 hours per day
  • They maintain a low body temperature of about 86°F-93°F
  • While considered warm blooded, Sloths have the largest variation in tollerable body temperatures
  • They move in and out of shade to regulate their body temperature
